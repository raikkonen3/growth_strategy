Problem & Opportunity

For a new user to become an active contributor, they need to move through three stages. Click a card to see the gap in each.

01

Arrival

Discovering and following a creator
New users don't know what to do after landing, or where to find a creator they're interested in. Matching and onboarding guidance aren't enough to help them find direction quickly.
→
02

Connection

Deciding to take the first step, the key decision point before Activation
Even after finding a creator of interest, users may hesitate to act, either lacking enough diamonds to send a gift, unlock content, or message, or lacking enough information to judge whether it's worth it (pricing, response speed, activity level).
→
03

Amplification

Whether a positive interaction sustains itself into becoming an active contributor
Even when a user has a positive interaction, the platform has no mechanism to carry that experience forward, whether into repeat visits or into growth for the platform.
Why it matters: These three stages are interdependent. A gap in any one limits the payoff from the other two; fixing only one won't make the whole journey work.
Opportunity: If the three stages formed a self-reinforcing loop, smoother arrival and connection would create more positive interactions. Some of those users become repeat, active contributors, and their positive experience lowers the cost of the next round of arrival and connection, so growth wouldn't need to restart from scratch each cycle.

Goal: Within 6 months, raise both the rate and quality of new users completing their first positive interaction (Activation), begin converting a portion of them into repeat, active contributors, and feed that outcome back into the start of the journey.
Scope: excludes long-term retention once users are established (spend depth, subscriptions, scaled referral)

Why These Three Stages

A new user's journey has many optimizable points, including search, recommendations, and external marketing. This strategy focuses on Arrival, Connection, and Amplification because neither has a systematic mechanism today, and together they form one complete growth path rather than isolated fixes.
